Phishing Email Analysis

In this project, I analyzed a phishing email to show how attackers exploit user trust, manipulate attention, and bypass basic filters. The goal was to break down the threat elements and offer insights for everyday users and IT teams.

Sample Phishing Email

From: jira-notification@secure-update.com
To: employee@example.com
Subject: Immediate Action Required: JIRA Account Password Reset

Dear Employee,

Our security system has detected unusual login attempts on your JIRA account. To protect your data, please reset your password within the next 24 hours.

Reset your password here:  
http://secure-update-jira.com/reset-password

Failure to reset your password will result in temporary suspension of your account.

Thank you for your prompt attention.

Best regards,  
JIRA Security Team

Objective

  • Understand phishing attack structure
  • Identify spoofed sender details and malicious URLs
  • Document social engineering techniques
  • Provide clear, actionable recommendations

Methodology

  1. Reviewed the email headers and sender domain for inconsistencies.
  2. Examined the link to check for suspicious redirects and mismatched URLs.
  3. Analyzed the language used to identify urgency and impersonation tactics.
  4. Compared message personalization and legitimacy cues.

Key Findings

  • Sender Spoofing: The sender domain secure-update.com is unrelated to the official JIRA domain.
  • Deceptive Links: The link directs to secure-update-jira.com, which is not a verified Atlassian site.

Lessons Learned

  • Always verify sender email domains, not just display names.
  • Hover over links to inspect actual URLs before clicking.
  • Be cautious of urgent language designed to pressure quick action.
  • Regular security awareness training helps users spot these red flags.
