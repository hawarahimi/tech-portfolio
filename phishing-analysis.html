Phishing Email Analysis

In this project, I analyzed a phishing email to show how attackers exploit user trust, manipulate attention, and bypass basic filters. The goal was to break down the threat elements and offer insights for everyday users and IT teams.

Objective

  • Understand phishing attack structure
  • Identify spoofed sender details and malicious URLs
  • Document social engineering techniques
  • Provide clear, actionable recommendations

Methodology

  1. Created a realistic phishing email sample.
  2. Inspected sender email headers and reply-to addresses.
  3. Hovered over and traced embedded links for redirection or mismatches.
  4. Recognized urgency, impersonation, and manipulation tactics.

Phishing Email Sample

From: "JIRA Notifications" <jira-notify@supportservices.com>
To: user@example.com
Subject: Action Required Immediately: Password Reset Request

Dear User,

We detected suspicious activity on your account. Please reset your password immediately to avoid suspension.

Click here for a password reset .

If you did not request this, please ignore this message.

Best Regards,
JIRA Support Team

Key Findings

  • Sender Spoofing: The sender’s email domain impersonated a trusted service but originated from an unrelated server.
  • Deceptive Links: URLs were disguised to appear legitimate, but redirected to suspicious destinations.
  • Social Engineering: The message used urgency (“Action Required Immediately”) and impersonated internal tools like JIRA.
  • Missing Personalization: The email lacked specific user references, a common phishing indicator.

Lessons Learned

  • Check email headers and don’t trust display names alone.
  • Hover over links and compare visible text to the actual URL.
  • Be cautious of fear-based or urgent language in messages.
  • Educate teams regularly on identifying suspicious communications.
